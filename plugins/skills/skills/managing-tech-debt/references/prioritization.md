# Prioritizing debt

The goal is a defensible order, not a precise score. Rank so the next person knows what to pay down
first without re-litigating every item.

## The lens: interest × likelihood
- **Interest** — what does this cost you *while it sits*? Debt that slows every change to a hot file
  has high interest; a cosmetic gap in a rarely-touched corner has low interest.
- **Likelihood** — how often does it actually bite? A scary-sounding item that triggers once a year
  ranks below a mild one that taxes every PR.
- Multiply (roughly). High interest + high likelihood = do it now. Low + low = carry it, maybe forever.

## Effort breaks ties
Among similarly-painful items, do the cheap ones first — quick wins compound and clear noise from the
register. A high-effort, high-interest item may need to be split into smaller scheduled chunks (and
each chunk becomes an issue).

## What not to do
- Don't rank by *recency* (the last thing someone complained about) or *annoyance* (the thing that
  bugs you personally). Those bias toward the loud, not the costly.
- Don't let the register become a graveyard — if an item has sat untouched for ages at low priority,
  either its interest is genuinely low (fine, mark it "carry") or you're mis-ranking it.

## Tie to reality
Re-rank when context changes: a "when multi-session matters" item jumps the moment multi-session
ships. Prioritization is a living judgment, not a one-time sort.
